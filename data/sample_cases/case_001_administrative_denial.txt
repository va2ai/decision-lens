DEMO REVIEW BOARD — ADMINISTRATIVE DECISION

Case Number: C-000-000-001
Claimant: Jane Demo
Date of Decision: 2024-08-15

ISSUE
Entitlement to compensation benefits for a chronic respiratory condition claimed
as related to occupational exposure during prior service.

DECISION
The claim for compensation benefits for chronic respiratory condition is DENIED.

EVIDENCE OF RECORD
1. Service occupational records, January 1998 through March 2004.
2. Private medical records from Demo Memorial Clinic, dated April 2018 through
   January 2024, documenting a diagnosis of mild chronic obstructive disease.
3. Claimant statement dated June 2024.
4. Demo Agency examination report dated July 2024.

REASONS AND BASES FOR DECISION
The claimant served in a covered occupation from 1998 to 2004 with documented
exposure to airborne particulates. Service records do not document any treatment
for or complaint of respiratory symptoms during the period of service. Private
medical records reflect first diagnosis of a chronic respiratory condition in
April 2018, approximately fourteen years after separation.

The Demo Agency examiner opined in July 2024 that the claimant's current
respiratory condition is "less likely than not" related to occupational exposure
during service. The examiner stated that the more likely cause is the claimant's
post-service occupational history, which included approximately eight years
of work in environments with secondary smoke exposure. The examiner also noted
that the gap between separation and first documented diagnosis weighed against
a service-related etiology.

The claimant submitted a personal statement asserting that respiratory symptoms
began intermittently during the final year of service, but the statement is not
corroborated by contemporaneous records or by a buddy statement from any
co-worker or family member. No private medical opinion linking the condition
to occupational exposure has been submitted.

The preponderance of the evidence is against the claim. The benefit-of-the-doubt
rule does not apply because the evidence is not in approximate equipoise.

CONCLUSION
Service connection for chronic respiratory condition is denied. The claimant
may submit new and relevant evidence — including a private medical nexus
opinion or contemporaneous lay statements from co-workers — and request
supplemental review within one year of this decision.
